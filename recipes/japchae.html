Japchae


Description

Japchae is a sweet potato noodle dish that is mixed with a variety of vegetables and meat. One of the most popular special occasion dishes in Korea, this easy to make (and gluten-free!) dish is a flavor explosion!

Ingredients

Steak Marinade:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 4 teaspoons mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teapsoon ground black pepper
  • 1 pound flank steak, sliced across the grain

Noodle Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 pinch ground black pepper
  • 8 ounces Korean sweet potato noodles (dangmyun)
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups baby spinach, or more to your taste
  • 1 teaspoon light olive oil
  • 1 large carrot, cut into matchsticks
  • 1/2 onion, sliced

Steps

  1. Combine 1/4 soy sauce, mirin, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, garlic, and 1 teaspoon pepper in a bowl. Add steak; stir to coat. Marinate for atleast 30 minutes.
  2. Combine 1/4 cup soy sauce, honey, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, and 1 pinch pepper in a large bowl; stir well.
  3. Fill a large pot with water and bring to a rolling boil; stir in noodles and return to a boil. Cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, for about 5 minutes. Drain. Add noodles to sauce; toss to coat.
  4. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add spinach and cook, uncovered, until just bright green, for about 1 minute. Drain in a colander and immediately immerse in ice water for several minutes to stop the cooking process. Drain.
  5. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add carrot and onion; saute until tender, for about 5 minutes. Stir in steak and marinade; cook until tender, for about 5 to 8 minutes. Stir in spinach. Add noddles and egg; cook and stir until well combined and heated through for about 2 minutes.
