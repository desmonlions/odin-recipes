Tteokbokki


Description

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean rice cake (garaetteok) dish, simmered in a sweet, savory spicy sauce that usually consists of gochujang, sugar and soy sauce. A beloved Korean street food staple, this dish is guaranteed to blow your mind.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups water
  • 2 dried anchovies, or add to your taste
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon corn syrup
  • 2 Korean fish cakes, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, thickly sliced
  • 1 spring onion, thickly sliced

Steps

  1. Combine water and anchovies in a saucepan; bring to a boil and cook for 10 minutes. Remove and discard anchovies; set anchovy water aside.
  2. Combine gochujang (chile paste), sugar, soy sauce, and corn syrup in a bowl.
  3. Add rice cakes, onion, and gochujang (chile paste) mixture to anchovy water; bring to a boil and boil for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add spring onion; boil 3 minutes more.
